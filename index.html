Drug Administration Routes

Drug administration routes refer to the ways in which drugs are introduced into the body. Understanding these routes is crucial for healthcare professionals to ensure safe and effective medication delivery.

Intravenous (IV) Administration

Intravenous administration involves delivering medication directly into the bloodstream through a vein. This route ensures rapid onset of action.

Intradermal Administration

Intradermal administration involves injecting medication into the dermis layer of the skin. This route is commonly used for skin tests.

Subcutaneous Administration

Subcutaneous administration involves injecting medication into the subcutaneous tissue layer beneath the skin. This route allows for slow and continuous absorption of medication.
